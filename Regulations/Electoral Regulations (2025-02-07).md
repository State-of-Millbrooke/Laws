<h1>Electoral Regulations of the State of Millbrooke</h1>

**Effective 7 February 2026**
  
</div align="center">


---

## INTERPRETATION AND APPLICATION

#### Definitions

1.	In this Regulation,
- "alternate account" means any Roblox account that is not the individual's primary account;
- "campaign period" means the period designated by the Chief Electoral Officer for the purposes of campaigning which ends at the close of final voting for the election, unless otherwise ordered;
- "candidate" means an individual whose nomination in relation to an election was approved by the Chief Electoral Officer;
- "Chief Electoral Officer" means the Chief Electoral Officer appointed under the Constitution;
- "election" includes a general election and a special election;
- "elector" means an individual who meets the qualifications to vote under the Constitution;
- "inducement" includes money, gift, valuable consideration, refreshment, entertainment, office, placement, employment and any other benefit of any kind and, for certainty, does not include merely transporting an individual to or from a voting opportunity;
- "intimidate" means
  - to use or threaten to use force, violence or restraint against an individual,
  - to inflict injury, harm, damage or loss on an individual or property or to threaten to do any of these, or
  - to otherwise intimidate an individual or to threaten to do anything that is otherwise intimidation of an individual;
- "nomination period" means the period established by the Chief Electoral Officer during which nominations for an election are accepted;
- "primary account" means the Roblox account an individual uses the majority of the time;
- "political party" means an organization that has as a primary purpose the fielding of candidates for any election;
- "special election" means an election other than one conducted as part of a general election;
- "voting" means to cast a ballot;
- "voting place" means the areas of and in the vicinity of the places established as a place where voting may occur during voting hours;
- "voting hours" means the hours established by the Chief Electoral Officer during which voting may occur;
- "writ of election" means, in relation to an election, a written order of the Chief Electoral Officer to conduct an election;

## CHIEF ELECTORAL OFFICER

#### Duties and powers
2.	The Chief Electoral Officer has the following duties in addition to all others established by this Regulation or by law:
- to provide guidance and supervision respecting the voting procedures and the conduct of elections and referenda;
- to provide information to the public regarding the voting procedures and election proceedings;
- to ensure that this Regulation, the Constitution, and all relevant laws are enforced.

## CANDIDATES

#### Who may be nominated

3.	To be qualified for nomination as a candidate for an election, an individual must:
- be an elector,
- nominate only using the individual’s primary account,
- not be disqualified from public office, 
- not be disqualified by the Chief Electoral Officer, 
- meet all eligibility requirements of the position for which the individual wishes to stand as a candidate, as prescribed by law, 
- not have violated any laws or regulations respecting elections, and
- not violate any laws or regulations respecting elections in the future.

#### Making nominations

4.	The timeline and means for making nominations shall be prescribed by the Chief Electoral Officer, in a writ of election.

#### When an individual is a candidate

5.	An individual becomes a candidate when the individual's nomination is approved by the Chief Electoral Officer.
6.	An individual continues to be a candidate until the certification of the election, unless the individual earlier withdrawals, or is declared to no longer be a candidate by the Chief Electoral Officer.
7.	A candidate must, at all times, remain eligible as a candidate as described in this Regulation.
8.	If a candidate, at any time, is no longer eligible as a candidate, the Chief Electoral Officer may declare the candidate to no longer be a candidate. 
9.	A disqualification under this Regulation is not void even if, after the fact, the individual is or becomes eligible as a candidate.

#### Withdrawal of candidate

10.	At any time up until 24 hours before the start of voting, a candidate may withdraw from the election by contacting the Chief Electoral Officer.

## VOTING

#### Each individual may vote only once in an election
11.	An individual must not vote more than once in the same election.

#### Voting to be by secret ballot

12.	Voting at an election must be by secret ballot.
13.	Each individual present at a voting place must preserve the secrecy of the ballot and, in particular, must not do any of the following:
- interfere with an individual who is voting;
- attempt to discover how an individual voted;
- communicate information regarding another individual's candidate choice; or
- induce an individual, directly or indirectly, to show the ballot in a way that reveals how the individual voted.

14.	A voter may not be required in any legal proceedings to reveal how the voter voted in an election.

#### Individuals who may be present at a voting place

15.	Except as provided in this Regulation, an individual must not be present at a voting place during voting hours.
16.	The following individuals may be present at a voting place during voting hours:
- the Chief Electoral Officer;
- peace officers;
- individuals who are present at the voting place for the purpose of voting; and
- other individuals permitted to be present at the voting place by the Chief Electoral Officer.

17.	Other than for the purpose of voting, a candidate must not be present at a voting place during voting hours.

#### Conduct at Voting Places

18.	There is absolutely no obstructing access to the voting places at any time.
19.	There is absolutely no campaigning at or in the vicinity of voting places.
20.	There is no loitering inside the voting places.
21.	During voting hours, voters are only allowed inside the building of a voting place to cast their vote, and must exit the building of a voting place as soon as their vote is cast.
22.	Observers or spectators are to remain outside the building of a voting place.
23.	When there is 60 minutes or less to the scheduled time when the polls close, individuals may observe inside the building of a voting place.

## ELECTION COMMUNICATIONS AND CONDUCT

#### When campaigning activities permitted

24.	Unless otherwise ordered in a writ of election, campaigning is permitted only:
- before the nomination period, 
- during the nomination period, and
- during the campaign period.

25.	A candidate, a candidate's staff, and a political party must not campaign for or against a candidate or a political party outside of the campaign hours established by the Chief Electoral Officer.

#### Political advertising authorization

26.	Political advertising is any advertising that promotes or opposes a political party, its leader or a candidate.
27.	Political advertising includes advertising about an issue that one or more political parties or candidates has taken a position on.
28.	All political advertising must include a spoken or written line stating who authorized the advertising, as follows:
- All candidates must include their name in their political advertising (e.g. “Authorized by the XYZ campaign”).
- Candidates affiliated with a political party must additionally include their affiliated party (e.g. “This advertisement was authorized by the XYZ political party").

29.	Electoral regulations respecting campaigning also apply to the content of political advertising.

#### Campaigning venues

30.	The Chief Electoral Officer may provide a venue for campaigning, but campaigning is not restricted to said venue, provided campaign activities comply with these Regulations.

#### Forms of campaigning prohibited

31.	Disruptive forms of campaigning are prohibited, including but not limited to:
- stalking while canvassing;
- spamming messages;
- campaigning in bad faith or maliciously;
- campaigning with ill intent;
- making misleading or false statements;
- making misrepresentations;
- being overtly disrespectful towards any candidate;
- conduct which endangers the public; and
- unlawful demonstrations.

32.	Unfair forms of campaigning are prohibited, including but not limited to:
- campaigning while on-duty as a public servant, peace officer, or government employee;
- taking unfair advantage of Discord administrative mechanics offered to certain roles;
- taking unfair advantage of in-game mechanics offered to certain roles; and 
- taking unfair advantage of in-game administrative functions offered to certain individuals.

## ELECTION OFFENCES

#### Enforcement

33.	The enforcement of these Regulations and relevant electoral laws is the duty of the Chief Electoral Officer.
34.	The Chief Electoral Officer may use the following mechanisms to enforce these Regulations and relevant electoral laws:
- issuance of a notice of contravention to the individual in violation,
- imposition of limitations on the individual in violation for the duration of an election,
- disqualification of the individual in violation from candidacy in an election,
- order that the individual in violation be disqualified from public office, and
- referral of the offence to the Attorney General for criminal prosecution.

#### Appeal

35.	The Supreme Court may hear and adjudicate appeals arising out of the decision or adjudication of the Chief Electoral Officer.

#### Vote buying

36.	An individual or organization must not pay, give, lend or procure inducement:
- to induce an individual to vote or refrain from voting,
- to induce an individual to vote or refrain from voting for or against a particular candidate, or
- to reward an individual for having voted or refrained from voting.

37.	An individual must not accept inducement:
- to vote or refrain from voting,
- to vote or refrain from voting for or against a particular candidate, or
- as a reward for having voted or refrained from voting.

38.	An individual or organization must not advance, pay or otherwise provide inducement, or cause inducement to be provided, knowing or with the intent that it is to be used for any of the acts prohibited by this Regulation.
39.	An individual or organization must not offer, agree or promise to do anything otherwise prohibited by this Regulation.
40.	An individual or organization prohibited from doing something by this Regulation must not do the prohibited act directly, indirectly or by another individual or organization on behalf of the individual or organization who is subject to the prohibition.

#### Intimidation

41.	An individual or organization must not intimidate or threaten an individual:
- to persuade or compel an individual to vote or refrain from voting,
- to persuade or compel an individual to vote or refrain from voting for or against a particular candidate, or
- to punish an individual for having voted or refrained from voting.

42.	An individual or organization must not, by abduction, duress, or fraudulent means,
- impede, prevent, or otherwise interfere with an individual's right to vote;
- compel, persuade, or otherwise cause an individual to vote or refrain from voting; or
- compel, persuade, or otherwise cause an individual to vote or refrain from voting for a particular candidate.

43.	An individual or organization prohibited from doing something by this Regulation must not do the prohibited act directly, indirectly or by another individual or organization on behalf of the individual or organization who is subject to the prohibition.

#### Offences in relation to nominations
44.	An individual or organization must not:
- purport to nominate an individual for an election without authority to do so, or
- publish or cause to be published a false statement that an individual had been nominated.

#### Offences in relation to withdrawals 

45.	An individual or organization must not:
- purport to withdraw a candidate from an election without authority to do so, or
- publish or cause to be published a false statement that a candidate has withdrawn.

#### Offences in relation to the administration of elections

46.	An individual must not impede or obstruct the Chief Electoral Officer, or any person acting on his or her direction, in performing duties and exercising powers under these Regulations, the Constitution, or other electoral laws.

#### False or misleading information

47.	An individual must not provide false or misleading information, including material misrepresentations, to the Chief Electoral Officer, or any person acting on his or her direction, in order to obtain something of value or interest that the individual is not entitled to obtain.

#### Complaints regarding contraventions

48.	If the Chief Electoral Officer receives a complaint alleging that this Regulation has been contravened, the Chief Electoral Officer must consider whether to investigate the matter.
49.	The Chief Electoral Officer may refuse to investigate a complaint that in the view of the Chief Electoral Officer appears to be frivolous, vexatious or obviously unfounded.
50.	If a complaint is made in writing and the Chief Electoral Officer decides not to conduct an investigation, the Chief Electoral Officer must notify the complainant in writing of the reasons for the decision.

#### Offences in relation to candidates and others

51.	A candidate bears responsibility for the actions and conduct of individuals acting under his or her direction.
52.	A candidate may be liable for any violations of these Regulations or electoral laws by an individual acting under the direction of said candidate.
53.	An individual who appears, in the discretion of the Chief Electoral Officer, to be ostensibly or genuinely acting on behalf of a candidate is be deemed to be acting under the direction of said candidate for the purposes of these Regulations.
54.	A candidate who knows or reasonably ought to know that an individual acting under his or her direction has violated, is violating, or will violate these Regulations or electoral laws has a responsibility to:
- notify that individual that his or her conduct offended, offends, or will offend a Regulation or electoral law;
- take adequate and reasonable steps to prevent the offence from occurring within the reasonable purview of the candidate;
- notify the Chief Electoral Officer as soon as the candidate foresees or reasonably ought to foresee that a violation of these Regulations or electoral laws is about to occur, has occurred, or will occur.

55.	Where an individual acting on behalf of a candidate is deemed to have violated these Regulations or electoral laws, he or she is subject to the same penalties as if the candidate violated the same Regulations or electoral laws.

## ADDITIONAL REGULATIONS

#### Only primary accounts may participate

56.	Only an individual's primary account may be a candidate or act as staff for a candidate.
57.	Upon the request of the Chief Electoral Officer, an individual who has nominated themselves to be a candidate, is a candidate, or is staff for a candidate must demonstrate to the Chief Electoral Officer that they are using their primary account.
58.	Where the Chief Electoral Officer has made a lawful request under this Regulation, received an adequate response, but is still not satisfied that an individual is in fact using their primary account, he or she may request advice from the Housing Authority.
59.	If an individual, upon being served a lawful request under this Regulation by the Chief Electoral Officer, fails to respond or refuses to demonstrate they are in compliance with this Regulation, that individual forfeits being a nominee to be a candidate, a candidate, or staff for a candidate, as the case might be.
60.	An individual is deemed to have failed to produce a response if they do not respond within 48 hours of a lawful request under this Regulation.
