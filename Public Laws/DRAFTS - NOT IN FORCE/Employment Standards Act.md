## INTERPRETATION AND APPLICATION

1.	This Act applies to all employees, prospective employees, and employers.
2.	In a matter concerning a person to which the *Public Service Employment Act* applies, that Act takes precedence should that Act conflict with this Act.
3.	This Act does not apply to the holder of an elected or judicial office in respect of that position.
4.	Where a person is employed in multiple roles or positions, this Act applies independently in respect of each role or position held by the person.
5.	In this Act, 

- “employee” includes a person who, in exchange for wages, does work for an employer; 
- “employer” includes:
  - an owner, proprietor, manager, superintendent, overseer, or receiver of an activity, business, trade, occupation, profession, or project who has control or direction of, or is directly or indirectly responsible for, the employment of a person in it; and
  - a department head, a senior public service employee, or an employee of the public service who has control or direction of, or is directly or indirectly responsible for, the employment of a person in any department of the public service;
- “work” includes:
  - performing work for an employer,
  - supplying services to an employer,
  - attending an employer’s premises,
  - attending training in relation to employment,
  - completing an examination or assessment in relation to employment,
  - reporting to an employer, and
  - representing an employer;
- “work resource” means any thing and any property intended to be used for a work purpose and notably includes:
  - digital and information technology infrastructure,
  - guidelines and procedures,
  - instructions,
  - intellectual property,
  - real property and the premises on which work is to be performed,
  - tools, 
  - training, and
  - vehicles or modes of transportation.

## EMPLOYMENT STANDARDS

#### Right to employment

6.	No person may be unreasonably refused employment.
7.	An employer has a duty to inform a person to whom the employer has refused employment of the reasons for said refusal with appreciable particularity and within a reasonable time.

#### Termination of employment

8.	An employee may be suspended or terminated from employment only for good cause.
9.	An employee may be laid off or dismissed from employment for any lawful reason with 14 days notice, except an employee to which the *Public Service Employment Act* applies.

#### Discrimination and other practices prohibited 

10.	No person may be discriminated against, in the context of their employment and in any respect connected to their employment, on the basis of any of the following:

- race;
- national or ethnic origin;
- colour;
- religion;
- age;
- sex or sexual orientation;
- disability;
- choice of the model of their body, including the usage of any torsos that includes characteristics from either sex; and
- choice of hair colour, unless the choice is not a naturally occurring hair colour.

11.	No person may be subject to oppression, coercion, or obstruction in the course of their employment.
12.	No person may be punished or retaliated against for reporting misconduct, harassment, abuse, or any unlawful act.

#### No treating as if not employee

13.	No employer may treat, for the purposes of this Act, a person who is an employee of the employer as if the person were not an employee under this Act. 

#### Hours of work

14.	No person may be required by his or her employer to work for the employer, including to attend or complete any training, examination, or assessment in relation to employment, for more than:

-	2 hours in any 24-hour period, and
-	6 hours per week.

15.	No employer may treat an employee negatively or differently for refusing or indicating a refusal to work hours in excess of that prescribed in this Act.
16.	Nothing in this Act precludes an employee from voluntarily working hours in excess of that prescribed in this Act.

#### Conditions of employment

17.	An employer may establish the following conditions of employment for a position:

-	Residency status;
-	Access to Roblox experiences or Discord servers related to the State of Millbrooke;
-	Arrests and convictions obtained during the period of employment; and
-	Professional or regulatory licensing requirements.

18.	An employer must give notice to any employee the employer considers to be in default of his or her conditions of employment.
19.	An employer may dismiss an employee who is in default of his or her conditions of employment for 5 or more consecutive days and who has not made an appreciable effort to resolve the issue.

#### Employment records

20.	Every person has the right to access their own employment records and make corrections to any incorrect information on those records.

## EMPLOYMENT CONTRACTS

#### In general

21.	In general, an employer and employee may contract into such mutually and freely agreed to terms which relate to employment standards or employment relations in an employment contract.

#### No contracting out

22.	No employer or employee may contract out of or waive anything in this Act and any such contracting out or waiver is void.

#### Greater contractual or statutory right

23.	If one or more provisions in an employment contract or in another Act that directly relate to the same subject matter in this Act provide a greater benefit to an employee than this Act, the provisions in the contract or Act apply and this Act does not apply.

#### Prohibited terms

24.	No employment contract may require an employee to submit or consent to a search, at the employer’s request, of the employee’s personal belongings or personal vehicle, except when the employee is arriving at, on, or leaving from the employer’s premises.
25.	No employment contract may require an employee to submit or consent to a search, at the employer’s request, of the employee’s personal belongings or personal vehicles not in the immediate vicinity of the employer’s premises.
26.	No employment contract may require an employee to submit or consent to a search, at the employer’s request, of the employee’s dwelling. 
27.	No employment contract may require an employee to surrender to the employer or to make available for work purposes, whether temporarily or otherwise, any personal belongings, personal vehicles, or the employee’s dwelling.

## EMPLOYER’S PROPERTY

#### Employer’s obligations

28.	An employer must provide an employee with the adequate and appropriate work resources to do the work required of the employee effectively.
29.	An employer may not treat an employee whose work performance is, in the opinion of the employer, unsatisfactory negatively or differently if the employee’s unsatisfactory work performance is, in part or in whole, due to a lack of adequate or appropriate work resources needed to do the work required of the employee effectively or otherwise through no fault of the employee.

#### Presumption

30.	In a legal proceeding where an employee’s unsatisfactory work performance is at issue, the judge or adjudicator must presume that the employee’s unsatisfactory work performance is through no fault of the employer and that the employee had adequate and appropriate work resources to do the work required of the employee effectively unless the employee demonstrates otherwise.

#### Employee’s obligations

31.	An employee must take all reasonable steps to safeguard and keep in good working order all work resources entrusted to the employee by the employer.

#### Refusing work resources

32.	An employer may refuse to provide an employee with a work resource, revoke an employee’s access to a work resource, and retrieve possession of a work resource if the employer has reason to believe and does believe that:

-	The employee has failed to report for work;
-	The employee has engaged in intentional, careless, or reckless conduct in breach of written policies or the law or conduct that is abusive or corrupt; 
-	The employee has damaged or lost a work resource as the result of negligence;
-	The employee has used, is using, or intends to use a work resource for an unlawful purpose; or
-	The employee has used, is using, or intends to use a work resource for a purpose other than a work purpose or for a purpose other than one which the employer has approved of.

## REMEDY

33.	Anyone whose entitlements, as provided in this Act, have been infringed or denied may apply to a court of competent jurisdiction to obtain such remedy as the court considers appropriate and just in the circumstances.
