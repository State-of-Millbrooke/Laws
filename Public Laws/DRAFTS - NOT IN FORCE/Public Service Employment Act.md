<h1>Public Service Employment Act</h1>

*Chapter -, Statutes of Millbrooke*

**Enacted - Month 202-**
  
</div align="center">

---
## INTERPRETATION AND APPLICATION

1.	In this Act,

- “applicant” means a person who is participating in a selection process;
- “department head” means, in respect of each department, the position named in Column B in each of the Schedules to this Act;
- “employee” means a person employed in the public service;
- “hierarchical level” means each distinct rank or level to which an employee may be appointed and includes only those which the Developers have provided a department;
- “public service” includes the departments named in Column A in each of the Schedules to this Act;
- “selection process” means a process in which persons may be considered for employment in the public service;
- “senior public service” includes the department head and the two hierarchical levels immediately below the department head,
  - except in the Millbrooke National Guard, where it includes the hierarchical levels at and above Lieutenant Colonel.

2.	Except as provided in this Act, applicants in a selection process and employees must not be discriminated against on the sole basis that they are employed in another department.
3.	The provisions of the *Employment Standards Act* apply to the public service.

## EMPLOYMENT RESTRICTIONS

#### In general

4.	An employee in any Schedule A department must not be concurrently employed in any other Schedule A department. 
5.	An employee in any Schedule B department may be concurrently employed in either one other Schedule B department or one Schedule A department.
6.	An employee may be concurrently employed in any amount of Schedule C departments.
7.	No person may be concurrently employed in more than one senior public service position.

#### Deemed employment

8.	A person employed as a volunteer firefighter or as a volunteer EMS shall have their employment in the Hennepin County Fire Department be deemed as employment in a Schedule C department for the purposes of employment restrictions.
9.	A judge or justice of any court is deemed to be employed in a Schedule B department for the purposes of employment restrictions. For greater certainty, the Chief Judge and Chief Justice do not occupy senior public service positions.

## LABOUR RELATIONS

10.	No employee or former employee in the 6 months after they cease to be an employee may create, participate in, maintain, or lobby for a union to represent and collectively bargain for the public service or any parts of it, unless all of the department heads concerned by the action consents to it, or unless the Assembly otherwise orders.

## SELECTION PROCESSES

#### Authorities and delegated authority

11.	The department head has exclusive authority to administer selection processes and to make appointments in that department and may delegate that authority to an employee of that department in writing.
12.	The department head is responsible for that department’s selection processes and appointments, including when an employee is exercising delegated authority.

#### Rights of applicants

13.	All applicants are entitled to fair and equal treatment in selection processes.
14.	All applicants are entitled to objectively fair assessment and grading of each component of their application and qualifications, including written responses, oral responses, and practical assessments conducted as part of the selection process.
15.	No department may maintain a system whereby it automatically eliminates applications, except insofar permitted by this Act.
16.	No applicant may be eliminated from a selection process except for any of the following reasons:

- The applicant does not meet the qualification standards for employment;
- The applicant’s application demonstrates less merit than at least one other applicant; 
- The applicant has been found to be untruthful, fraudulent, or dishonest in the selection process; or
- The applicant does not satisfy the conditions of employment.

17.	An applicant has the right to be informed of the reasons, with appreciable particularity, for being eliminated from a selection process within a reasonable time after all the applicants in that selection process have been assessed.

#### Conditions of employment

18.	A department head may prescribe conditions of employment for a selection process independently of the merit of an applicant, the quality of an application, or the qualifications of an applicant.
19.	A condition of employment may only extend to the following:

- Convictions in the last 10 years;
- Arrests in the last 2 years and for which a pardon has not been granted;
- Citations in the last 6 months and for which a pardon has not been granted;
- Administrative or civil monetary penalties in the last 6 months;
- Dishonourable discharges from the public service in the last 2 years;
- Residency in the State of Millbrooke; or
- Eligibility to receive a security status or clearance.

20.	A department head who eliminates a candidate for not satisfying one or more conditions of employment must notify the applicant of each of the grounds therefor as soon as possible and keep a dated record of the same.

## REMEDY AND ENFORCEMENT

21.	Anyone whose rights, as provided in this Act, have been infringed or denied may apply to a court of competent jurisdiction to obtain such remedy as the court considers appropriate and just in the circumstances.
22.	If the Attorney General finds a person in contravention of this Act, he or she may issue a Notice of Contravention to that person and to demand that person to comply with this Act.
23.	The Attorney General may charge a person in contravention of this Act for Disobeying a statute under section 47 of the *State Code* if that person continues to be in contravention 5 days after a Notice of Contravention was served upon that person.
