*Chapter 6, Statutes of Millbrooke*

**Enacted 29 June 2026**

## INTERPRETATION AND APPLICATION

#### Definitions

1.	In this Act,

-	“conveyance” means a motor vehicle, a vessel, or an aircraft;
-	“defence establishment” means any building, structure, grounds, area, and other real property ordinarily used by the National Guard that has been so designated, and the materiel and other things situated in or on any such building, structure, grounds, area, and other real property;
-	“enrol” means to cause any person to become an officer or member of the National Guard;
-	“materiel” means all property other than real property provided for the National Guard or for any purpose under this Act, and includes conveyances, arms, ammunition, provisions, and equipment so provided;
-	“member” means a person, other than an officer, who is enrolled in or attached to the National Guard;
-	“military police officer” means an officer or member who is so designated, and holds peace officer status;
-	“officer” means a person who holds a commission in the National Guard;
-	“service offence” means an offence under this Act committed by a person while subject to this Act;
-	“superior officer” means any officer or member who, in relation to any other officer or member, is by this Act, or by regulations or custom of the National Guard, authorized to give a lawful command to that other officer or member;
-	“termination” has the same meaning as in the [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md) and includes a release under dishonourable or disgraceful circumstances;
-	“unit” means an individual body of the National Guard that is organized as such pursuant to this Act;

#### Interpretation

2.	The words in this Act, unless a definition is provided in this Act, shall be interpreted:

-	Firstly according to its ordinary meaning; 
-	Secondly according to any applicable meaning that may be assigned to those same words in the [*State Code*]( https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/State%20Code);
-	Thirdly according to any applicable meaning that may be assigned to those same words by custom of the National Guard or of the armed forces; and 
-	Thereafter according to applicable common law rules.

#### Power to make regulations

3.	The Governor may make regulations for the organization, training, discipline, efficiency, administration, and good government of the National Guard and generally for carrying the purposes and provisions of this Act, and of the [Constitution]( https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/Constitution#national-guard) as it relates to the National Guard, into effect.
4.	Subject to the regulations made by the Governor, the Adjutant General may make regulations for the organization, training, discipline, efficiency, administration, and good government of the National Guard and generally for carrying the purposes and provisions of this Act, and of the [Constitution]( https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/Constitution#national-guard) as it relates to the National Guard, into effect.
5.	The power to make regulations in this Act includes the power to require all officers and members to abide by a code of conduct.
6.	The power to make regulations in this Act includes the power to make the regulations in equivalent terminology to that in this Act.

#### Subject to the *Public Service Employment Act*

7.	The National Guard and all officers and members, except for the reserve force, are subject to the [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md).

## THE NATIONAL GUARD

### REGULAR FORCE

#### Constitution

8.	The regular force consists of the officers and members who are enrolled for continuing, full-time military service.
9.	The regular force may be organized into such units and other elements as the Adjutant General may determine from time to time.

#### Command

10.	The authority and powers of command of officers and members may be prescribed in regulations.

#### Enrolment and release

11.	The enrolment of a person binds the person to serve in the National Guard until the person is, in accordance with regulations, lawfully released.

### MILITARY POLICE

#### Appointment

12.	The Adjutant General may designate any member of the regular force eligible to hold peace officer status as a military police officer.

#### Jurisdiction

13.	The military police has jurisdiction:

-	on a defence establishment and over persons present at a defence establishment,
-	over materiel and conveyances of the National Guard, 
-	over officers and members, and 
-	at any place where the military police or National Guard or any part of it is lawfully present.

14.	When the National Guard is activated by the Governor, the military police has jurisdiction over all the State of Millbrooke, subject to the directions of the Adjutant General.

#### Enforcement

15.	A military police officer may use as much force as is reasonably necessary in the circumstances to enforce this Act and regulations made under this Act, including to investigate contraventions of the same.

#### Detention and custody

16.	A military police officer may detain and take into custody a person subject to service offences in this Act:

-	who he or she finds committing a service offence;
-	who, on reasonable grounds, he or she believes has committed a service offence; or
-	who, on reasonable grounds, he or she believes is about to commit a service offence.

17.	This Act does not limit the power of a military police officer to detain or arrest a person under an authority in the [*State Code*]( https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/State%20Code), subject only to the jurisdiction of the military police.

#### Special duty obligations – activation 

18.	When the National Guard is activated by the Governor, a military police officer must only act as a peace officer for the National Guard and may not act as a peace officer in any other capacity.
19.	When the National Guard is activated by the Governor, no department head other than the Adjutant General may:

-	require a military police officer who is concurrently employed in that department to report for duty in a department other than the National Guard; or 
-	administer any measures under the [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md) in respect of that military police officer for not reporting for duty.

### RESERVE FORCE

#### Constitution

20.	The Adjutant General may create a reserve force and enrol any number of volunteers therein at a rank not exceeding corporal.

#### Derogation

21.	The [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md) and the [*Employment Standards Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-4%5D%20Employment%20Standards%20Act.md) do not apply to the reserve force and its members.
22.	The Adjutant General has exclusive authority to make regulations on any subject matter ordinarily dealt with in the [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md) in respect of the reserve force and its members.

#### Command

23.	Any officer or member of the regular force may be designated as a superior officer in respect of any member of the reserve force.

#### Restrictions

24.	A member of the reserve force may not report for or be on duty except to attend a training or when the National Guard is activated, or unless otherwise ordered by the Adjutant General.
25.	A member of the reserve force may not be designated a military police officer.

## DEFENCE ESTABLISHMENTS

#### Designation

26.	The Developers may designate any building, structure, grounds, area, or other real property ordinarily used by the National Guard as a defence establishment.

#### Search without warrant

27.	A military police officer may search without a warrant any person and the personal vehicle of any person who is arriving at, presenting for entry to, present on, leaving from, or attempting to leave a defence establishment.
28.	A military police officer may search without a warrant any conveyance arriving at, being presented for entry to, present on, leaving from, or attempting to leave a defence establishment.
29.	A military police officer may search without a warrant any container or receptacle affixed to a conveyance of the National Guard or to a defence establishment.

#### Special powers

30.	A peace officer, officer, or member is justified in using force that is intended or is likely to cause death or grievous bodily harm against a person who:

-	Appears to be scaling a fence, wall, or barrier around the exterior of a defence establishment in an attempt to unlawfully gain entry to a defence establishment;
-	Appears to be attempting to gain entry to a defence establishing without presenting for inspection;
-	Is unlawfully present on or in a defence establishment; or
-	Points a firearm or imitation firearm in the direction of a defence establishment.

31.	A peace officer, officer, or member is justified in using force that is intended or is likely to damage, destroy, or set fire to property if it appears that:

-	A conveyance is about to unlawfully gain entry to a defence establishment;
-	A conveyance has entered a defence establishment without having been presented for inspection; or 
-	A conveyance in the vicinity of a defence establishment poses an imminent risk to a defence establishment.

## DISCIPLINE AND SERVICE OFFENCES

#### Application of the *Public Service Employment Act*

32.	Nothing in this Act derogates from any authority of the department head of the National Guard under the [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md).
33.	In addition to the grounds and authorized measures for performance management, disciplinary action, and termination under the [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md), persons subject to service offences in this Act may also be dealt with in accordance with this Act.

#### Persons subject to service offences

34.	The following persons are subject to service offences in this Act:

- An officer or member of the regular force;
- A member of the reserve force who is
  - on duty,
  - in uniform,
  - undergoing training, whether in uniform or not,
  - present, whether in uniform or not, at any drill or training,
  - in or on any conveyance of the National Guard, 
  - in or on any defence establishment,
  - called into or on active service, or
  - serving with any unit that is on active service;
- A person, not otherwise subject to service offences, who accompanies any unit that is on active service in any place if the person
  - participates with that unit in the carrying out of any of its movements, manoeuvres, duties, or operations,
  - is accommodated by that unit at any place, or
  - is embarked on a conveyance of that unit; 
- An alleged spy for the enemy; and
- A person, not otherwise subject to service offences, who agreed to be so subject.

35.	Every person subject to service offences continues to be so subject in respect of any act or omission done by that person during such time that the person is subject to service offences notwithstanding that the person may have, since that act or omission, ceased to be a person subject to service offences.

#### Sanctions for service offences 

36.	Every person who commits a service offence is liable to:

- if the person is an officer or member, termination and disqualification from enrolling or receiving a commission in the National Guard for up to 2 years from the date of termination, or to less sanction; or
- in any other case, disqualification from enrolling or receiving a commission in the National Guard for up to 2 years from the date of the service offence.

37.	In this Act, “less sanction”, as it relates to sanctions for service offences, includes the following:

-	Direction to undergo training;
-	Direction to participate in a specific program or activity;
-	Direction to complete written work;
-	Close supervision;
-	Formal warning or reprimand;
-	Permanent reassignment or involuntary transfer to another unit;
-	Removal, restriction, or modification of duties for up to 6 months;
-	Permanent demotion;
-	Suspension for up to 30 days;
-	Ineligibility to participate in an internal selection process for lateral moves, intra-departmental transfers, and promotions for up to 6 months; and
-	Any combination of the above sanctions.

#### Authorities and delegated authority

38.	The Adjutant General has exclusive authority to investigate an officer or member who:

-	is alleged to have committed a service offence, 
-	appears to be committing a service offence, or 
-	appears will commit a service offence.

39.	If the Adjutant General is satisfied, on a balance of probabilities, that an officer or member has committed a service offence, the Adjutant General may administer any sanctions permitted under this Act that he or she considers appropriate in the circumstances upon that officer or member.
40.	The Adjutant General may delegate the authority to investigate and administer sanctions to any officer in writing.

### SERVICE OFFENCES

#### Misconduct of command

41.	Every superior officer in command of a conveyance, defence establishment, or unit of the National Guard who

- when under orders to carry out an action or operation, does not bring the officers and members, conveyances, or other materiel under his or her command into action, 
- being in action, improperly withdraws from action, or
- being in action, improperly abandons or surrenders any officer or member, conveyance, defence establishment, or other materiel under his or her command,

  commits an offence. 

#### Misconduct in action

42.	Every person who

- when under orders to carry out an action or operation, fails to carry the orders into effect,
- being in action, improperly withdraws from action, or
- improperly abandons or surrenders any conveyance, defence establishment, or other materiel,

  commits an offence.

#### Misconduct in presence of enemy

43.	Every person who

- goes over to the enemy,
- assists the enemy with materiel, or
- improperly does or omits to do anything that results in the capture by the enemy of persons or the capture or destruction by the enemy of materiel,

  commits an offence.

#### Misconduct to imperil or cause prejudice

44.	Every person who

- does or omits to do anything with intent to imperil the success of the National Guard,
- does or omits to do anything with intent to prejudice the security of the National Guard, or
- improperly occasions false alarms,

  commits an offence.

#### Security offence

45.	Every person who

- improperly holds communication with or gives intelligence to the enemy,
- without authority discloses in any manner or makes known to any person not entitled to receive it any information relating to the numbers, position, materiel, movements, preparations for movements, operations, or preparations for operations of the National Guard, or
- without authority discloses in any manner or makes known to any person not entitled to receive it any information relating to a publication, document, password, procedure, information technology system, or identification signal of the National Guard,

  commits an offence.

#### Operational offence

46.	Every person who

- does violence to any person cooperating with the National Guard,
- without orders from the person’s superior officer, improperly destroys or damages any property,
- breaks into any house or other place in search of plunder,
- steals from, or with intent to steal searches, any place or person, or
- steals any money or property that has been left abandoned, exposed, or unprotected,

  commits an offence.

#### Spies for the enemy

47.	Every person who spies for the enemy commits an offence.

#### Mutiny

48.	Every person who

- causes a mutiny,
- conspires with any other person to cause a mutiny,
- joins in a mutiny,
- endeavours to persuade any person to join in a mutiny,
- being present, does not use his utmost endeavours to suppress a mutiny, or
- being aware of an actual or intended mutiny, does not without delay inform his superior officer thereof,

  commits an offence.

#### Insubordination

49.	Every person who disobeys a lawful command of a superior officer commits an offence.
50.	Every person who strikes or attempts to strike, draws or lifts up a weapon against, or uses, attempts to use, or offers violence against a superior officer commits an offence.
51.	Every person who uses threatening or insulting language to, or behaves with contempt toward, a superior officer commits an offence.

#### Resisting or escaping from custody

52.	Every person in lawful custody who resists or uses or offers violence to any person whose duty it is to apprehend the person commits an offence.
53.	Every person in lawful custody who breaks out of the place in which the person is confined commits an offence.

#### Absence without leave

54.	Every person who

- without authority leaves his or her place of duty,
- without authority is absent from his or her place of duty, or
- having been authorized to be absent from his or her place of duty, fails to return to his or her place of duty at the expiration of the period for which the absence of that person was authorized,

  commits an offence.

55.	Every person who knowingly makes a false statement to obtain or prolong a leave of absence commits an offence.

#### Stealing

56.	Every person who fraudulently and without colour of right takes any materiel of the National Guard with intent to deprive, temporarily or absolutely, the owner of it commits an offence.

#### Disgraceful conduct

57.	Every person who strikes or otherwise ill-treats any person who, by reason of rank or appointment, is subordinate to him or her commits an offence.

#### Prejudicing good order or discipline

58.	Every person who does any act or omission to the prejudice of good order and discipline commits an offence.
59.	An act or omission is to the prejudice of good order and discipline if it is in contravention of:

-	any of the provisions of this Act,
-	the [Constitution]( https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/Constitution),
-	any regulations made under this Act, 
-	any instructions published for the general information and guidance of the National Guard, or
-	any general, unit, or other orders.
