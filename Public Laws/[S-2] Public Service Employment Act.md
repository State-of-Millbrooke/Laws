<h1>Public Service Employment Act</h1>

*Chapter 2, Statutes of Millbrooke*

**Enacted 5 June 2026**

## INTERPRETATION AND APPLICATION

1.	In this Act,

- “administrative dismissal” means the dismissal of an employee under training;
- “applicant” means a person who is participating in a selection process;
- “department head” means, in respect of each department, the position named in Column B in each of the Schedules to this Act;
- “disciplinary action” means the measures taken for cause to address the misconduct of an employee, but does not include termination;
- “employee” means a person employed in the public service and includes employees under training;
- “employee under training” means an employee occupying the first hierarchical level in a department who is undergoing a training, apprenticeship, or developmental program in that department;
- “hierarchical level” means each distinct rank or level at which an employee may be employed or appointed and includes only those which the Developers have provided a department with;
- “interim measures” means the certain measures taken in the interim to minimise the risk to the department while the department head deals with the misconduct or alleged misconduct of an employee;
- “involuntary separation” means the dismissal of an employee as the result of an approved workforce adjustment process;
- “misconduct” means intentional, careless, or reckless conduct in breach of written policies or this Act or conduct that is abusive or corrupt;
- “peace officer” has the same meaning as in the [*State Code*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/State%20Code);
- “performance management” means a process intended to address an inability to perform the duties of one’s position to a reasonable standard;
- “public service” includes the departments named in Column A in each of the Schedules to this Act;
- “selection process” means a process in which persons may be considered for employment in the public service, including internal processes for lateral moves, intra-departmental transfers, inter-departmental transfers, and promotions;
- “senior public service” includes the department head and the two hierarchical levels immediately below the department head,
  - except in the Millbrooke National Guard, where it includes the hierarchical levels of and above Lieutenant Colonel;
- “termination” means the dismissal of an employee for cause and does not include resignations, administrative dismissals, and involuntary separations;
- “workforce adjustment" means the process for issuing involuntary separations due to lack of work, the discontinuance of a function, or a restructuring in a department.

2.	Except as provided in this Act, applicants in a selection process and employees must not be discriminated against on the sole basis that they are employed in another department.
3.	The provisions of the [*Employment Standards Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-4%5D%20Employment%20Standards%20Act.md) apply to the public service.
4.	A person with delegated authority under this Act may not sub-delegate that authority unless the department head who delegated that authority consents to a sub-delegation in writing.

## EMPLOYMENT RESTRICTIONS

#### In general

5.	An employee in any Schedule A department must not be concurrently employed in any other Schedule A department. 
6.	An employee in any Schedule B department may be concurrently employed in either one other Schedule B department or one Schedule A department.
7.	An employee may be concurrently employed in any amount of Schedule C departments.
8.	No person may be concurrently employed in more than one senior public service position.
9.	No person may be employed in the public service unless they are at least 16 years of age.

#### Deemed employment

10.	A person employed as a volunteer firefighter or as a volunteer EMS shall have their employment in the Hennepin County Fire Department deemed as employment in a Schedule C department for the purposes of employment restrictions.
11.	A person employed at a hierarchical level of or above Second Lieutenant in the Millbrooke National Guard shall have their employment in that department deemed as employment in a Schedule B department for the purposes of employment restrictions.
12.	A judge or justice of any court is deemed to be employed in a Schedule B department for the purposes of employment restrictions. For greater certainty, the Chief Judge and Chief Justice do not occupy senior public service positions.

## LABOUR RELATIONS

13.	No employee may create, participate in, maintain, or lobby for a union to represent and collectively bargain for the public service or any parts of it, unless all of the department heads concerned by the action consents to it, or unless the Assembly otherwise orders.

## SELECTION PROCESSES

#### Authorities and delegated authority

14.	The department head has exclusive authority to administer selection processes and to make appointments in that department and may delegate that authority to any senior public service employee of that department in writing.
15.	The department head is responsible for that department’s selection processes and appointments, including when an employee is exercising delegated authority.

#### Administering selection processes

16.	The department head may determine the appropriate parameters and eligibility criteria of selection processes based on the staffing needs of the department, subject to this Act.
17.	Any selection process must allow at least 24 hours for any person qualified to apply to do so.
18.	A department head who intends to hire employees under training or to staff positions at the first hierarchical level or the hierarchical level immediately above employees under training must administer a selection process open to and advertised to the public for that purpose.
19.	A department head who intends to effect lateral moves, inter-departmental transfers, or promotions not involving a senior public service position must use an advertised selection process open to and advertised to its employees at some or all hierarchical levels in that department for that purpose.
20.	A department head may appoint an employee in a department to a senior public service position in that department only, without an advertised selection process. 

#### Rights of applicants

21.	All applicants are entitled to fair and equal treatment in selection processes whereby all equally qualified or equally meritorious applicants are treated in a like manner.
22.	All applicants are entitled to objectively fair assessment and grading of each component of their application and qualifications, including written responses, oral responses, and practical assessments conducted as part of the selection process.
23.	No department may maintain a system whereby it automatically eliminates applications, except insofar as permitted by this Act.
24.	No applicant may be eliminated from a selection process except for any of the following reasons:

- The applicant does not meet the qualification standards for employment;
- The applicant’s application demonstrates less merit than at least one other applicant; 
- The applicant has been found to be untruthful, fraudulent, or dishonest in the selection process; or
- The applicant does not satisfy the hiring conditions.

25.	If the department head eliminates an applicant from a selection process under section 24 for a lack of qualifications or a lack of merit, that department head must also eliminate all equally qualified or unqualified and all equally meritorious or unmeritorious applicants in accordance with the principle stated in section 21.
26.	An applicant has the right to be informed of the reasons, with appreciable particularity, for being eliminated from a selection process within a reasonable time after all the applicants in that selection process have been assessed.

#### Hiring conditions

27.	A department head may prescribe hiring conditions for a selection process independently of the merit of an applicant, the quality of an application, or the qualifications of an applicant.
28.	A hiring condition may only extend to the following:

- Convictions in the last 10 years;
- Arrests in the last 2 years and for which a pardon or record suspension has not been granted;
- Citations in the last 6 months and for which a pardon or record suspension has not been granted;
- Administrative or civil monetary penalties in the last 6 months;
- Terminations from the public service in the last 2 years;
- Disciplinary actions issued in the last 6 months;
- Ongoing performance management; or
- Residency in the State of Millbrooke.

29.	A department head who eliminates a candidate for not satisfying one or more hiring conditions must notify the applicant of each of the grounds therefor as soon as possible and keep a dated record of the same.

## TRAINING PROGRAMS

#### Authorities and delegated authority

30.	The department head has exclusive authority to administer training, apprenticeship, or developmental programs in that department and may delegate that authority to any employee of that department in writing.
31.	The department head is responsible for that department’s training, apprenticeship, or developmental programs, including when an employee is exercising delegated authority.

#### Rights of employees under training

32.	All employees under training are entitled to:

- Objectively fair access, treatment, assessment, and examination at each step of the training, apprenticeship, or developmental program;
- Be informed of the expectations on all employees under training;
- Be informed of the expected standards and passing criteria of the training, apprenticeship, or developmental program;
- Reasonable levels of supervision and instruction as part of the training, apprenticeship, or developmental program; and
- A reasonable period of time to meet all required standards and complete all required assessments and other components.

#### Rejection during training

33.	The department head may reject an employee under training if, based on a fair and objective assessment, the department head determines that:

- In the last 30 days, the employee under training has made no or minimal progress in the training, apprenticeship, or developmental program through no fault of the department head;
- The employee under training has not satisfactorily completed an assessment or examination or a component thereof that all employees under training were subject to;
- The performance of the employee under training is such that he or she could have been placed on performance management; or
- The employee under training has been administered disciplinary action while in the training, apprenticeship, or developmental program in that department.

34.	A department head who has rejected an employee under training may do any of the following:

-	Allow the employee under training to continue or redo the training, apprenticeship, or developmental program;
-	Allow the employee under training to redo an assessment or examination or a component thereof; or
-	Administratively dismiss the employee under training.

35.	A rejection during training and an administrative dismissal are not forms of disciplinary action or a termination and may not be used, disguised, or recorded as disciplinary action or a termination.

#### For greater certainty

36.	For greater certainty, an employee who has been directed to undergo training or to undertake some other measure as part of performance management or as the result of disciplinary action is not an employee under training for the purposes of this Act.

#### Conversion of employee under training status

37.	An employee under training ceases to be an employee under training once he or she has completed the training, apprenticeship, or developmental program to the satisfaction of the department head.
38.	If a department head establishes a probationary period for employees under training in that department, an employee under training in that department ceases to be an employee under training once he or she has completed the probationary period.

## PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

#### Authorities and delegated authority

39.	The department head has exclusive authority to administer performance management in that department and may delegate that authority to any employee of that department in writing.
40.	The department head is responsible for that department’s use of performance management, including when an employee is exercising delegated authority.

#### Grounds for performance management

41.	Performance management may be administered where the employee has failed to report for work or remain on duty in a manner prescribed by the department head.
42.	Performance management may be administered where the employee continues to make mistakes, errors, or omissions which are not the result of any misconduct.
43.	Performance management may be administered where:

- The level of job performance required was communicated to the employee;
- Reasonable levels of supervision and instruction were provided to the employee;
- The employee was allowed a reasonable period of time to meet the required level of job performance;
- The employee has failed to meet the required level of job performance; and
- The employee’s ongoing level of job performance continues to be unsatisfactory.

#### Authorized measures

44.	A department head may take the following measures as part of performance management, or a combination thereof:

- Direction to undergo training;
- Direction to participate in a specific program or activity;
- Direction to complete written work;
- Close supervision;
- Reassignment;
- Modification of duties; and
- Any other measures jointly agreed to by the employee and the department head.

45.	When administering performance management to an employee, the department head must notify that employee of the grounds, under this Act, giving rise to performance manage that employee.
46.	Any measures taken as part of performance management must pertain to the grounds giving rise to performance management.
47.	Performance management is temporary in nature and any measures taken as part of performance management expire as soon as the grounds giving rise to performance manage the employee no longer exist.
48.	A department head may not begin or continue to performance manage an employee whose ongoing performance is not or is no longer demonstrably unsatisfactory.
49.	Performance management is not a form of disciplinary action and may not be used, disguised, or recorded as disciplinary action.

## DISCIPLINARY ACTION

#### Authorities and delegated authority

50.	The department head has exclusive authority to administer disciplinary actions in that department and may delegate that authority to any senior public service employee of that department in writing.
51.	The department head is responsible for that department’s use of disciplinary actions, including when an employee is exercising delegated authority.

#### Grounds for disciplinary action

52.	Disciplinary action may be administered where, on a balance of probabilities, the employee has engaged in misconduct with respect to:

- Conduct that is likely to bring the public service or the administration of justice into disrepute;
- Conduct that is likely to compromise public confidence in the public service;
- Conduct that a reasonable person would view as being disrespectful;
- Conduct which gives rise to an actual, apparent, or potential conflict of interest between the employees’ professional responsibilities and private interests;
- Demonstrably discriminatory actions or decisions which treat a person or group negatively based on race, national or ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, or disability;
- Directing any person to engage in misconduct or to commit an unlawful act, whether or not the employee had the lawful authority to issue that direction;
- Engaging in political activities on duty;
- Failure to carry out a lawful order or direction;
- Failure to intervene in face of misconduct by another employee, where it would have been reasonably expected of the employee to do so;
- Failure to report the misconduct of another employee;
- Failure to respect the parameters of the employee’s legal authority under the law or otherwise acting without lawful authority; 
- Failure to safeguard confidential information coming into the employee’s knowledge as the result of employment;
- Failure to uphold the rights of others;
- Favouritism or retaliation;
- Misuse, theft, unauthorized use, unauthorized modification, or unauthorized destruction of government property;
- Misuse, theft, unauthorized use, unauthorized modification, or unauthorized destruction of other property coming into the employee’s possession by reason of or in relation to his or her employment in the public service;
- Neglect of duty which is intentional, careless, or reckless, and beyond a simple performance issue;
- Providing false, misleading, or inaccurate statements in the context of employment with intent to deceive or as the result of negligence, except in cases of honest mistake;
- Using force in excess of what is reasonably necessary in the circumstances;
- Using the status of employee in the public service to obtain any improper advantage or benefit; or
- Violation of any law, act, regulation, statutory instrument, or enactment.

#### Authorized measures

53.	A department head may administer the following measures as disciplinary action, or a combination thereof:

- Direction to undergo training;
- Direction to participate in a specific program or activity;
- Direction to complete written work;
- Close supervision;
- Formal warning or reprimand;
- Permanent reassignment or involuntary transfer to another work unit; 
- Removal, restriction, or modification of duties for up to 6 months;
- Permanent demotion;
- Suspension for up to 30 days; 
- Ineligibility to participate in an internal selection process for lateral moves, intra-departmental transfers, and promotions for up to 6 months; and
- Any other measures jointly agreed to by the employee and the department head.

54.	When administering disciplinary action to an employee, the department head must notify that employee of the grounds, under this Act, giving rise to issue disciplinary action to that employee.
55.	No disciplinary action may be administered more than 30 days after the date of the conduct giving rise to grounds to administer disciplinary action to that employee.
56.	Any records of disciplinary actions in respect of an employee must be destroyed 6 months after it was issued.

- Except where one or more disciplinary actions were issued within 6 months of any previous disciplinary actions on the same employee in the same department, in which case all records of disciplinary actions in that department in respect of that employee may be retained for 6 months after that employee’s most recent disciplinary action in the said department, after which the records must be destroyed, provided no further disciplinary actions have been issued to that employee in the said department. 

57.	An employee may not be issued disciplinary action multiple times for a single occurrence of misconduct, except where that misconduct is ongoing.
58.	The department head must ensure proportionality, procedural fairness, and consistency in administering disciplinary actions.
59.	The department head must consider and weigh mitigating, aggravating, and other relevant factors in the circumstances in administering disciplinary actions.

## TERMINATION

#### Authority

60.	The department head has exclusive authority to issue terminations in that department and may not delegate that authority.

#### Grounds for termination

61.	An employee may be terminated for inactivity where the employee has been on performance management for that reason for at least 30 days within the last 60 days, remains on performance management for that reason, and has not made an appreciable effort to resolve the issue.
62.	An employee may be terminated for unsatisfactory performance where the employee has been on performance management for that reason for at least 30 days within the last 60 days, remains on performance management for that reason, and has not made an appreciable effort to resolve the issue.
63.	An employee may be terminated for misconduct where the employee has engaged in two or more instances of misconduct within the last 6 months for which the employee received disciplinary action.
64.	An employee may be terminated for serious misconduct where the employee has engaged in serious misconduct for which the employee could have received disciplinary action. Misconduct may qualify as serious misconduct:

- Where the conduct significantly erodes the public confidence in the department or the public service;
- Where the conduct is characterized by multiple aggravating factors which are not mitigated;
- Where the employee has deliberately acted in bad faith;
- Where it is demonstrable that the employee is likely to reoffend; or 
- Where there is a particularly strong need to denounce the misconduct and deter others from engaging in similar misconduct; 

65.	An employee may be terminated for criminality where it appears that, on a balance of probabilities, the employee has committed a misdemeanour or felony offence under the [*State Code*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/State%20Code), whether or not the employee has been arrested, charged, or convicted of an offence.

#### Authorized measures

66.	When terminating an employee, the department head must notify that employee of the grounds, under this Act, giving rise to terminate that employee.
67.	No termination may be issued more than 4 months after the date of the conduct giving rise to grounds to terminate that employee.

- For the purposes of this section, in a termination for criminality except where mandatory termination under this Act applies, the earliest of the following dates apply:
  - Date of the misconduct;
  - Date of the arrest;
  - Date of the laying of the charges; and
  - Date of conviction or sentencing.

68.	Any records of terminations in respect of an employee must be destroyed 2 years after it was issued.
69.	The department head must ensure proportionality, procedural fairness, and consistency in administering disciplinary actions.
70.	The department head must consider and weigh mitigating, aggravating, and other relevant factors in the circumstances in issuing a termination.

#### Mandatory termination

71.	A department head must terminate an employee who is no longer a resident of the State of Millbrooke or whose residency status has been revoked by the Housing Authority.
72.	A department head must terminate an employee whose account has been permanently banned from any Roblox experiences or Discord servers related to the State of Millbrooke by a Developer or a delegate.
73.	A department head must terminate an employee who is sentenced to a term of imprisonment of any length as the result of being convicted of a misdemeanour or felony offence under the [*State Code*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/State%20Code).

#### Deemed termination 

74.	An employee who resigns with a record of disciplinary action within the last 6 months is deemed to have been terminated, unless the department head otherwise orders.
75.	An employee who resigns during an ongoing investigation for misconduct in which that employee is the subject of the investigation is deemed to have been terminated, unless the department head otherwise orders.
76.	An employee who resigns may be retroactively deemed by the department head to have been terminated if it is later determined that:

-	In the 4 months prior to that employee’s resignation, that employee engaged in misconduct for which he or she could have been terminated; or
-	In the 30 days prior to that employee’s resignation, that employee engaged in misconduct for which he or she could have received disciplinary action.

## INTERIM MEASURES

#### Authority

77.	The department head has exclusive authority to administer interim measures in that department and may not delegate that authority.

#### Grounds for interim measures

78.	Interim measures may be administered where there is a perceived or actual risk to the department as the result of an employee’s misconduct or alleged misconduct and the department head believes that administering interim measures would mitigate that risk.

#### Authorized measures

79.	A department head may administer the following interim measures, or a combination thereof, in respect of the employee alleged to have engaged in misconduct:

- Removal, restriction, or modification of duties for up to 10 days; and
- Administrative suspension for up to 10 days.

80.	If the department head believes that an employee’s misconduct or alleged misconduct is likely to be repeated by one or more other employees, the department head may administer interim measures which affect some or all employees of that department by removing, restricting, or modifying the duties of any employee or group of employees for up to 10 days.
81.	When administering interim measures, the department head must notify any employees or group of employees affected of the grounds, under this Act, giving rise to administer interim measures on those employees or group of employees.
82.	Any interim measures taken must pertain to the grounds giving rise to administer interim measures.
83.	Interim measures are temporary in nature and any interim measures expire as soon as the grounds giving rise to the interim measures no longer exist or 10 days after the interim measures took effect, whichever comes first.
84.	A department head may not begin or continue to administer interim measures where there are no or are no longer grounds for doing so.
85.	Interim measures are not a form of disciplinary action and may not be used, disguised, or recorded as disciplinary action.

## PUBLIC SERVICE STAFFING TRIBUNAL

#### Composition

86.	The Public Service Staffing Tribunal is comprised of the Chief Judge and judges appointed to the Public Service Staffing Tribunal by the Chief Judge.

#### Administrative proceeding for misconduct

87.	A department head or the Attorney General may refer a case of alleged misconduct or misconduct to the Public Service Staffing Tribunal for adjudication if the department head or Attorney General:

- Is unable to determine, on a balance of probabilities, whether or not an employee has engaged in misconduct;
- Is unable to obtain sufficient evidence to properly investigate the alleged misconduct of an employee;
- Is able to determine that misconduct has occurred but unable to reach a conclusion as to whether or not to administer disciplinary action, what disciplinary action to administer, or whether or not to terminate an employee; or
- Otherwise believes that a referral to the Public Service Staffing Tribunal for adjudication would be advisable.

88.	In an administrative proceeding for misconduct, the Public Service Staffing Tribunal has the same authority as the District Court in summoning witnesses, issuing subpoenas, and ordering the production of documentary evidence.
89.	In an administrative proceeding for misconduct, the Public Service Staffing Tribunal may administer interim measures of any length, until the conclusion of the administrative proceeding.
90.	At the conclusion of an administrative proceeding for misconduct, the Public Service Staffing Tribunal may do any of the following:

- Adjudicate that misconduct occurred;
- Adjudicate that misconduct did not occur;
- Administer disciplinary action;
- Terminate an employee;
- Modify or rescind disciplinary action or a termination already administered; and
- Make any other orders which the Public Service Staffing Tribunal deems appropriate in the circumstances.

#### Workforce adjustment

91.	A department head may request a workforce adjustment if the department head determines that a position is no longer required due to a lack of work, the discontinuance of a function, or a restructuring in that department, and that involuntary separations will necessarily be required as a result.
92.	Schedule A departments are not eligible to undergo workforce adjustment.
93.	To initiate the workforce adjustment process, the department head must do all of the following:

- Notify all employees occupying the affected positions that they are at risk of being involuntarily separated; and
- Refer an administrative proceeding for workforce adjustment to the Public Service Staffing Tribunal.

94.	To undergo workforce adjustment, the department head must satisfy the Public Service Staffing Tribunal that:

- There is a valid operational reason for undergoing workforce adjustment;
- The positions identified as being affected were identified logically and for a valid operational reason independently of the persons occupying those positions;
- There were attempts to minimise the number of affected positions;
- There has been a good faith effort to identify alternate work for employees occupying affected positions;
- The use of the workforce adjustment process does not constitute disguised discipline, whether intentional or not; and
- On the whole, the use of workforce adjustment to issue involuntary separations would not be unjust.

95.	At the conclusion of an administrative proceeding for workforce adjustment, the Public Service Staffing Tribunal may do any of the following:

- If satisfied that the department head’s request for workforce adjustment is lawful, approve workforce adjustment in full or in part and notify the employees occupying the affected positions that the department head is entitled to involuntarily separate some or all of them 30 days after the date of the notice; or
- If not satisfied that the department head’s request for workforce adjustment is lawful, reject the request for workforce adjustment and notify the employees occupying the affected positions that their positions are no longer affected and that they are no longer at risk of being involuntarily separated.

## REMEDY AND ENFORCEMENT

96.	Anyone whose rights, as provided in this Act, have been infringed or denied may apply to a court of competent jurisdiction to obtain such remedy as the court considers appropriate and just in the circumstances.
97.	The Attorney General’s oversight and inquiry authority under the [Constitution]( https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/Constitution) extends to the public service in respect of this Act.
98.	Every person who contravenes this Act is guilty of Disobeying a statute under section 47 of the [*State Code*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/State%20Code).
