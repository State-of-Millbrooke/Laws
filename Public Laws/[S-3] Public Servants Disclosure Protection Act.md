<h1>Public Servants Disclosure Protection Act</h1>

*Chapter 3, Statutes of Millbrooke*

**Enacted 12 June 2026**

## INTERPRETATION AND APPLICATION

#### Definitions

1.	In this Act,

- “department head” has the same meaning as in the [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md);
- “employee” has the same meaning as in the [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md);
- “protected disclosure” means a disclosure made in good faith of evidence or information related to any wrongdoing or alleged wrongdoing;
- “public service” has the same meaning as in the [*Public Service Employment Act*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/blob/main/Public%20Laws/%5BS-2%5D%20Public%20Service%20Employment%20Act.md);
- “wrongdoing” includes:
  - an act or omission that creates a substantial and specific threat to the public safety or security of persons,
  - a violation of any act or regulation by an employee,
  - gross mismanagement by an employee, 
  - reprehensible wrongdoing by an employee, and
  - abuse of authority by an employee.

## PROTECTED DISCLOSURE

#### Right to make protected disclosure

2.	An employee or former employee may make a protected disclosure to the Attorney General or to the Assembly of Millbrooke.

#### No criminal or civil liability for making protected disclosure

3.	An employee or former employee who makes a protected disclosure is not guilty of an offence and may not be liable for a civil action in relation to that protected disclosure.

#### Retaliation prohibited

4.	An employee or former employee who makes a protected disclosure may not be discriminated against or be subject to any adverse consequences, in the context of their employment and in any respect connected to their employment, present or future, for the sole reason of having made a protected disclosure.

#### Unlawful to reveal identity

5.	No person may reveal, seek to reveal, or compel any person to reveal the identity of any person who makes a protected disclosure or any person who cooperates or participates in a protected disclosure.

#### Protections for similarly situated persons

6.	Any person who cooperates with the Attorney General or the Assembly of Millbrooke in an investigation related to a protected disclosure is entitled to the same protections as the person who made the protected disclosure.
7.	Any person who is targeted or who is at imminent risk of being targeted for the sole reason that they participated or cooperated in a protected disclosure is entitled to the same protections as the person who made the protected disclosure.

#### No contracting out

8.	No person is competent to contract out of the obligations and protections provided in this Act. Any contractual clause that frustrates the protections for anyone who makes a protected disclosure, whether as its purpose of by its effect, is null and void.

## ENFORCEMENT

#### Offence

9.	Every person who contravenes this Act is guilty of Disobeying a statute under section 47 of the [*State Code*](https://github.com/State-of-Millbrooke/Laws/tree/main/State%20Code).

#### Powers of Attorney General

10.	The Attorney General may conduct investigations in relation to the substance of protected disclosures and in relation to contraventions of this Act, and may apply to a court of competent jurisdiction for a warrant or production order to further that investigation.
11.	Notwithstanding any other Act, the Attorney General may compel a department head to take such action as the Attorney General considers necessary to ensure that a person who makes a protected disclosure does not suffer adverse consequences, in the context of their employment and in any respect connected to their employment, present or future, for the sole reason of having made a protected disclosure, including:

-	An order to refrain from retaliation;
-	An order to set aside administrative action;
-	An order to reinstate a former employee; and
-	An order suspending an employee.

#### Remedy

12.	Anyone whose rights, as provided in this Act, have been infringed or denied may apply to a court of competent jurisdiction to obtain such remedy as the court considers appropriate and just in the circumstances.

#### Judicial review

13.	Any order made under this Act may be reviewed by a court of competent jurisdiction.
