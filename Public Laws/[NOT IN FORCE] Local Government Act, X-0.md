<h1>Local Government Act</h1>

*Chapter -, Statutes of Millbrooke*

**Enacted - Month 202-**

## INTERPRETATION AND APPLICATION

1.	This Act is enacted under section 216 of the Constitution. It is expressly declared that this Act comes into force immediately, that this Act retains supralegislative status until otherwise ordered by a Developer, and that this Act may not be amended or repealed by the Assembly except on the recommendation of a Developer.
2.	This Act provides for the legal framework and foundation for the establishment of local governments.
3.	This Act applies to and in respect of each municipality incorporated under this Act.
4.	All the powers granted to municipalities and their councils in this Act may also be exercised by the Assembly.
5.	Where the public interest may so require, all the powers granted to municipalities and their councils in this Act may be suspended by the Assembly by law for a maximum of 30 days at a time.
6.	If a power granted to municipalities and their councils in this Act is to be exercised by the Assembly, the Assembly is subject to the rules and laws binding on the Assembly in that exercise of powers, but not those binding on municipalities and their councils.

## MUNICIPAL INCORPORATION PROCESS

7.	The Assembly may, by an act of incorporation, incorporate an area under its jurisdiction into a new municipality.
8.	A municipality may be incorporated as a village, town or city.
9.	An act of incorporation must specify
-	the name of the new municipality;
-	the boundaries of the area to be incorporated into the new municipality and said boundaries are deemed to be the boundaries and jurisdiction of the new municipality; and
-	the classification of the new municipality as a village, town or city.

10.	If an existing municipality or any part of it is located inside an area to be incorporated, the Assembly must either
-	dissolve the existing municipality by repealing its act of incorporation before incorporating the new municipality; or 
-	specify in the act of incorporation that the existing municipality will be amalgamated with the new municipality.

## POWERS AND JURISDICTION OF MUNICIPALITIES

#### Fundamental powers and limits on powers

11.	A municipality has the capacity, rights, powers and privileges of a natural person of full capacity.
12.	The governing body of a municipality is its Council.
13.	A reference to the Council of a municipality or to the members of a Council is a reference to the mayor and the councillors of that municipality sitting together.
14.	Except as restricted by this Act, the powers of a Council may be exercised either by ordinance or by resolution.
15.	An ordinance may not be amended by resolution.
16.	The following acts of a Council must be done by adopting a municipal ordinance:
-	the exercise of legislative authority under this Act, and
-	the appointment of an official.

17.	Once an ordinance is lawfully adopted by a Council, it takes effect immediately, subject to such conditions or timelines contained in that ordinance.
18.	A provision of an ordinance has no effect if it is inconsistent with an enactment of the Assembly.
19.	A municipality and its Council may only exercise or perform their powers, duties and functions within the boundaries of the municipality except when two or more municipalities, by ordinance adopted by the Council of each participating municipality, establishes an intermunicipal scheme in relation to one or more matters for which they have authority under this Act.
20.	In this Act, the powers to regulate, prohibit and impose requirements, as applicable, in relation to a matter:
-	are separate powers that may be exercised independently of one another;
-	include the power to regulate, prohibit and impose requirements, as applicable, respecting persons, property, things and activities in relation to the matter;
-	include the power to provide that persons may engage in a regulated activity only in accordance with the rules established by ordinance;
-	include the power to prohibit persons from doing things with their property; and
-	include the power to require persons to do things with their property, to do things at their expense and to provide security for fulfilling a requirement.

21.	A municipality must make available to the public, on request, a statement respecting the Council’s reasons for adopting an ordinance that exercises the power to regulate, prohibit or impose requirements, as applicable, in relation to a matter.
22.	A municipality must act in the interests of the residents of its area including:
-	providing for good government of its community;
-	providing for services, laws and other matters for community benefit;
-	providing for stewardship of the public assets of its community; and
-	fostering the economic, social and environmental well-being of its community.

23.	A Council may, by ordinance, regulate, prohibit and impose requirements in relation to the following:
-	municipal services, including utility;
-	public places, including persons, property, things and activities that are in, on or near public places;
-	all uses of or involving a highway, roadway or a portion of a highway or roadway, including temporarily restricting the right of passage by the public or temporarily restricting or prohibiting all or some types of traffic;
-	firecrackers, fireworks and explosives;
-	bows and arrows, knives, firearms and other weapons;
-	the protection of the health, safety or protection of persons or property;
-	the protection and enhancement of the well-being of its community;
-	nuisances, disturbances and other objectionable situations that is liable to disturb the quiet, peace, rest, enjoyment, comfort or convenience of individuals or the public;
-	the erection, placing, alteration, maintenance, demolition and removal of signs, sign boards, advertisements, advertising devices and structures
-	graffiti and unsightly conditions on property;
-	indecency and profane, blasphemous or grossly insulting language;
-	public health, including unsanitary conditions on property;
-	parks and the natural environment;
-	animals and animal control including the seizure of animals unlawfully at large and the destruction of dangerous animals;
-	buildings and other structures, including as it relates to fire safety;
-	zoning of any land and the types of buildings and structures that may be constructed on zoned land;
-	businesses and commercial activity; and
-	the carrying on of a noxious or offensive business activity.

24.	A Council and other administrative bodies are deemed to continue in existence notwithstanding any change in their membership, and proceedings begun by one Council or administrative body may be continued and completed by a succeeding Council or administrative body of that municipality.

#### Ancillary powers

25.	A municipality may provide any service that the Council considers necessary or desirable and may do this directly or through another public authority or another person or organization.
26.	A Council may, in regulating under this Act, provide for a system of licences, permits or approvals, including by doing one or more of the following:
-	prohibiting any activity or thing until a licence, permit or approval has been granted;
-	providing for the granting and refusal of licences, permits and approvals;
-	providing for the effective periods of licences, permits and approvals;
-	establishing terms and conditions that must be met for obtaining, continuing to hold or renewing a licence, permit or approval, or providing that such terms and conditions may be imposed, the nature of the terms and conditions and who may impose them;
-	providing for the suspension or cancellation of licences, permits and approvals for failure to comply with a term or condition of a licence, permit or approval or failure to comply with the ordinance; and
-	providing for reconsideration or appeals of decisions made with respect to the granting, refusal, suspension or cancellation of licences, permits and approvals.

27.	A regulatory ordinance may provide for regulation by the use of permits and may do one or more of the following:
-	establish and impose a fee for a permit;
-	provide for the effective period of a permit;
-	establish terms and conditions of a permit;
-	establish terms and conditions that must be met for obtaining, continuing to hold or renewing a permit;
-	provide that terms and conditions for a permit may be imposed, the nature of the terms and conditions and who may impose them; and
-	provide for the suspension, revocation, cancellation, or forfeiture, for cause, of any permit which may be granted in the exercise of its powers.

28.	An ordinance providing for an intermunicipal scheme may do one of more of the following:
-	join with another municipality to exercise a power conferred on the municipality by this Act;
-	provide that the ordinances of one or more of the participating municipalities in relation to the matters dealt with by the scheme apply in other participating municipalities;
-	provide that the municipal powers, duties and functions of one or more of the participating municipalities may be exercised in relation to the scheme in another participating municipality; 
-	restrict a participating municipality from separately exercising its authority in relation to the matters dealt with by the scheme; and
-	establish the process by which a participating municipality may withdraw from the scheme.

29.	An ordinance providing for an intermunicipal scheme is not valid until each the Council of municipality has adopted an ordinance providing for an intermunicipal scheme.
30.	Where an intermunicipal scheme is lawfully in place, the powers of one municipality may extend beyond the boundaries of that municipality to the extent described and agreed to in the ordinances of each municipality.
31.	If the person presiding at a Council meeting considers that another person at the meeting is acting improperly, the person presiding may order that the person is expelled from the meeting.
-	If a person who is expelled does not leave the meeting, a peace officer may enforce the order under this section as if it were a court order.

32.	A Council, for its own information, may submit for the opinion of the electors any question with which the Council has or desires to have the power to deal.
-	A question under this section must be submitted to the Chief Electoral Officer.
-	The Chief Electoral Officer must cause a referendum to be held on the question submitted by Council.
-	The procedure for a referendum applies to a question submitted under this section.

33.	A Council may, as occasion requires, provide for the revision and consolidation of the ordinances of the municipality or any of them.

## THE COUNCIL

#### Size and quorum of councils

34.	The Council of a village is to consist of a mayor and two councillors, and the quorum for the Council is two members.
35.	The Council of a town is to consist of a mayor and four councillors, and the quorum for the Council is three members.
36.	The Council of a city is to consist of a mayor and eight councillors, and the quorum for the Council is five members.
37.	Should the number of members of a Council who may exercise their powers and carry out their duties is reduced to less than a quorum, the Assembly may, by an enactment, do one of the following:
-	order that the reduced number of members constitutes a quorum until an election is held; or
-	appoint qualified persons to act as councillors until an election is held, including when the vacant seat is that of the mayor.

#### Term of office for mayor and councillors

38.	The term of office for mayor and councillors elected at a general local election or at any election other than general local election:
-	begins after the Chief Electoral Officer certifies the person’s election and that person takes the oath or affirmation of office; and
-	ends immediately before the Chief Electoral Officer certifies the results of the next general local election.

39.	The term of office for a councillor appointed pursuant to this Act:
-	begins on the date indicated in the enactment appointing that person to the office of councillor and that person takes the oath or affirmation of office; and
-	ends immediately before the Chief Electoral Officer certifies the results of the special election in respect of that councillor’s seat, or of the next general local election, whichever is sooner.

40.	Notwithstanding any other provision, the term of office for a councillor appointed pursuant to this Act for the purpose of acting in place of a councillor on a leave of absence ends when the councillor on a leave of absence returns.
41.	If a mayor or councillor is the subject of a recall election and is not re-elected at the recall election, the term of office for that mayor or councillor ends immediately before the Chief Electoral Officer certifies the results of that recall election.

#### Oath or affirmation of office

42.	A mayor or councillor elected or appointed to office must take the following oath or affirmation of office before a judge or the Chief Electoral Officer: 

```
I, A.B., swear or solemnly affirm that I am qualified to hold the office of mayor/councillor.
I will faithfully perform the duties of my office to the best of my ability and will not allow any private interest to influence my conduct in public matters. 
I understand and accept the responsibility entrusted to me to exercise my functions with fairness, transparency, and respect for all persons.
```

43.	If, after 30 days of a person’s election or appointment to the office of mayor or councillor he or she has not yet taken his or her oath or affirmation of office, his or her election or appointment is deemed to be void.

#### Requirement for Council to sit regularly

44.	The Council of a municipality must meet to conduct business:
-	within 30 days of the certification of a local general election; and
-	at least once every 30 days thereafter.

45.	If a Council of a municipality fails to meet in accordance with this Act, the Assembly may, by an enactment, dissolve that Council and require the Chief Electoral Officer to hold a local general election for that Council.
46.	If a mayor or councillor is absent from Council meetings for a period of 30 consecutive days, or 4 consecutive regularly scheduled Council meetings, whichever is the longer period, the person is deemed to have resigned and a vacancy is deemed to occur.
-	This section does not apply to persons on a leave of absence, for the duration of their leave of absence.

#### Procedures governing Council proceedings
47.	In general, the powers and jurisdiction of a municipality are wholly exercisable by its Council.
48.	Unless a Council otherwise orders by ordinance, the mayor is the chair of all its proceedings.
49.	In general, a Council may decide the rules and procedures governing its proceedings by ordinance.
50.	A Council must, by ordinance, do all of the following before proceeding to business other than that referred to in this section:
-	establish the procedures that are to be followed for the conduct of its business, including the manner by which resolutions and ordinances may be adopted;
-	establish the procedures that are to be followed in conducting meetings of committees of Council;
-	establish the time and place of regular meetings of Council;
-	provide for the conduct of Council members at meetings of the Council and its committees;
-	determine a quorum for meetings of committees of Council; and
-	provide for the minutes or other records of the business transacted by the Council and its committees, in addition to the minutes required to be made under this Act.

#### Public meetings, notice, and closed meetings

51.	Council and committee meetings must be open to the public, except as provided in this Act.
52.	A Council must provide advance public notice in writing respecting the time, place and date of Council and committee meetings of at least 24 hours before the scheduled time of that meeting.
53.	A Council or committee may reschedule its meetings with public notice given as soon as feasible.
54.	A Council must not vote on, adopt, amend, repeal or otherwise alter an ordinance except by an ordinance adopted at a regular Council meeting for which advance public notice in writing was lawfully given.
55.	A Council must not vote on, adopt, amend, repeal or otherwise alter an ordinance when its meeting is closed to the public.
56.	A part of a Council or committee meeting may be closed to the public if the subject matter being considered relates to or is one or more of the following:
-	personal information about an identifiable individual who holds or is being considered for a position as an officer, employee or agent of the municipality or another position appointed by the municipality;
-	personal information about an identifiable individual who is being considered for an award or honour, or who has offered to provide a gift to the municipality on condition of anonymity;
-	labour relations or other employee relations;
-	law enforcement, if the Council considers that disclosure could reasonably be expected to harm the conduct of an investigation under or enforcement of an enactment;
-	litigation or potential litigation affecting the municipality;
-	an administrative tribunal hearing or potential administrative tribunal hearing affecting the municipality, other than a hearing to be conducted by the Council or a delegate of Council;
-	the receipt of advice that is subject to solicitor-client privilege, including communications necessary for that purpose;
-	information supplied, implicitly or explicitly, in confidence that would reveal trade secrets of a third party, or commercial, financial, labour relations, scientific or technical information of or about a third party, the disclosure of which could reasonably be expected to harm the third party;
-	a matter that, under another enactment, is such that the public may be excluded from the meeting; and
-	the consideration of whether a Council meeting should be closed to the public.

57.	If all or part of a meeting is closed to the public, the Council may nonetheless allow any person it considers appropriate to be present.
58.	Before holding a meeting or part of a meeting that is to be closed to the public, the Council must state, by resolution adopted in a public meeting,
-	the fact that the meeting or part is to be closed, and
-	the basis under the applicable section of this Act on which the meeting or part is to be closed.

59.	The minutes of a meeting or part of a meeting that is closed to the public must record the names of all persons in attendance.
60.	A Council or committee meeting may be conducted by means of electronic or other communication facilities, provided that all of the following requirements are satisfied: 
-	the facilities must enable the meeting's participants to hear, or watch and hear, the meeting; and
-	except for any part of the meeting that is closed to the public, the facilities must enable the public to hear, or watch and hear, the meeting.

#### Committees of Council 

61.	A Council may provide for such committees as it sees fit and may refer any matter to a committee for report.
62.	The following persons are eligible to be appointed to sit on a committee:
-	members of the Council, and
-	employees of the municipality.

63.	A Council may delegate, by ordinance, with or without restrictions or conditions, to any committee any of the executive or administrative powers exercisable by a Council.
64.	All committees of a Council are discharged immediately before a local general election.

#### Voting at Council proceedings

65.	Each member of the Council is entitled to vote on a question being put at a meeting at which they are present at the time of that vote.
66.	In this Act, a voting member means a member of the Council present at a meeting of the Council at the time of a vote and who:
-	is not disqualified from that vote, and
-	did not abstain for that vote.

67.	When a question on the adoption of an ordinance, resolution or order before a Council is put to a vote, that vote is:
-	carried, if a majority of voting members are for the question; or
-	defeated, if a majority of voting members are against the question.

68.	The person recording the minutes of a meeting of the Council must record, in respect of each question and vote, all of the following:
-	the question being put;
-	the names of members voting for and against that question;
-	the names of members disqualified from voting; and
-	the names of members abstaining from voting.

69.	For greater certainty, if the votes of the members of the Council present at the meeting at the time of the vote are equal for and against a question, the question is defeated and the presiding member shall so declare.
70.	If a member of the Council abstains from voting on a question, they:
-	must not be recorded as having voted either for or against that question, and
-	are deemed to not be a voting member for the purpose of determining if a question is carried or defeated.

71.	A member who has voted and who wishes to change or verify his or her vote may do so by addressing the clerk or chair of the proceedings, provided that the Council has not moved to a different question from that which the member wishes to change or verify his or her vote on.

#### Conflicts of interest

72.	Mayors, councillors and persons holding public office:
-	must not allow any private interest to influence his or her conduct in public matters; 
-	must disclose any direct or indirect conflict of interest he or she has in all matter; and 
-	must not participate in the discussion, vote or adjudication of any matters in which he or she has a direct or indirect conflict of interest.

73.	A member of a Council is not entitled to participate in the discussion of a matter or to vote on a question in respect of a matter, if the member has a conflict of interest in respect of that matter, as follows:
-	a direct or indirect pecuniary interest in the matter; or
-	another interest in the matter that constitutes a conflict of interest.

74.	If a member of a Council attending a meeting considers that the member has a conflict of interest in respect of a matter, the member must make a declaration of a conflict of interest and state the reason why the member considers this to be the case.
75.	When a member of a Council makes a declaration of a conflict of interest under this Act,
-	the person recording the minutes of the meeting must record the member’s declaration of a conflict of interest and the member’s statement of reasons for the declaration; and
-	the member making the declaration of a conflict of interest is disqualified from voting on that question.

76.	If a member of a Council makes a declaration of a conflict of interest under this Act or if a judge makes an order finding that a member of a Council has a conflict of interest, that member must not do any of the following:
-	remain or attend at any part of a meeting during which the matter for which the member has a conflict of interest is under consideration;
-	participate in any discussion of the matter at such a meeting;
-	vote on a question in respect of the matter at such a meeting; or
-	attempt in any way, whether before, during or after such a meeting, to influence the voting on any question in respect of the matter.

77.	A member of a Council must not use the member’s office to attempt to influence in any way a decision, recommendation or action to be made or taken by any other person or body if the member has a personal, direct or indirect pecuniary interest in the matter to which the decision, recommendation or other action relates.
78.	A member of a Council must not, directly or indirectly, accept a fee, gift or personal benefit that is connected with the member’s performance of the duties of office.
79.	A member of a Council or a former member must not use information that was obtained in the performance of the member’s office and not available to the general public for the purpose of gaining or furthering a direct or indirect pecuniary interest of the member or former member.
80.	A person who contravenes this Act with respect to conflicts of interest is disqualified from holding public office, unless the contravention was done inadvertently or because of an error in judgment made in good faith.
81.	A court may order a person who has contravened this Act with respect to conflicts of interest and realized financial gain in relation to that contravention to pay to the municipality an amount equal to all or part of the person’s financial gain as specified by the court.

#### Leaves of absences

82.	When a member of a Council is on a leave of absence, that member continues to hold office but must not exercise a power or carry out a duty as a Council member,

83.	A Council may grant a leave of absence by resolution to a member of the Council on the request of that member for any reason it deems appropriate.

84.	A member of a Council who is charged with a misdemeanour or felony offence under the _State Code_ is deemed to be on a leave of absence for the period beginning on the date of the charge, summons or undertaking and ending ending:
-	if the member is acquitted of the offence, on the day after the date of the acquittal;
-	if the member is convicted of the offence, on the date of the conviction;
-	if the member is discharged of the offence, on the day after the date of the discharge;
-	if all proceedings in relation to the charge are stayed, on the day after the date of the stay; or
-	if the charge is withdrawn, on the day after the date of the withdrawal.

## MAYOR AND DEPUTY MAYOR

85.	The Mayor is the chief executive officer of the city and the president of the Council.
86.	The Council may appoint a Councillor to be Deputy Mayor and may confer upon the Deputy Mayor any of the powers vested in the Mayor.
87.	The Deputy Mayor holds that office and the office of Councillor concurrently.
88.	The Mayor may:
-	oversee and inspect the conduct of departments and employees of the municipality or under the jurisdiction of the Council, 
-	give directions to departments employees of the municipality consistent with law, and
-	recommend to the Council such measures as the Mayor deems expedient.

## MUNICIPAL OFFICIALS

#### Departments and employees of the municipality

89.	A Council may establish and equip such departments and offices as it may from time to time deem expedient in the exercise of its powers, and may assign such functions and duties to the persons employed in such departments and offices as the Council may from time to time decide, including but not limited to the following:
-	municipal clerks,
-	license inspectors,
-	business inspectors,
-	budling inspectors,
-	a public works department,
-	a fire department,
-	a police department,
-	a health department, and
-	a park board.

90.	A Council may provide for the appointment, suspension, and removal of such employees as may be considered necessary, and shall require that their appointment, promotion, and change of status be based on merit and fitness.

## ELECTORS AND ELECTIONS

#### Qualifications 

91.	The same qualifications to be elector under the Constitution applies to general local elections.
92.	Only an individual’s primary account may be a candidate or act as staff for a candidate.

#### Arrangement for local general elections

93.	The Chief Electoral Officer administers all local elections and referendums.
94.	A general local election for the mayor and all councillors in each municipality must be held at each of the following times:
-	after the coming into force of this Act, at a time determined by the Developers;
-	within 30 days of the coming into force of the act of incorporation of the respective municipality; and
-	in every fourth month after a local general election.

95.	A general local election for the mayor and all councillors in a municipality whose Council has been dissolved by the Assembly in a manner provided in this Act.

#### Vacancies and special elections

96.	A vacancy is deemed to occur in any of the following circumstances:
-	a person elected to the office of mayor or councillor dies before taking office;
-	a person elected to the office of mayor or councillor is disqualified from holding public office before taking office or while in office;
-	a person holding the office of mayor or councillor resigns;
-	a person holding the office of mayor or councillor dies;
-	a person holding the office of mayor or councillor is removed from office;
-	a person holding the office of mayor or councillor is disqualified from holding public office; or
-	the office of mayor or councillor is declared vacant by a judge.

97.	For greater certainty, a vacancy does not occur when a mayor or councillor is on a leave of absence.
98.	Except as permitted under this Act, a special election must be held in the event of a vacancy to fill the vacant office if that vacancy occurs 30 or more days before a general local election is scheduled to occur.
99.	A Council may, by ordinance, decide that a special election is not to be held if the number of remaining councillors is at least one greater than the quorum for that Council.
